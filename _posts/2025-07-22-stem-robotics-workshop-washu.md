Conducted two hands-on robotics workshops for students aged 7–17 at the McKelvey School of Engineering, Washington University in St. Louis.

The sessions introduced students to robotics concepts and real-world applications. Each participant received a STEM kit to build and control their own robot, fostering creativity and problem-solving skills.

It was rewarding to see the enthusiasm and engagement of the students, with participants showcasing innovative ideas during the activities.

Initiatives like these play an important role in inspiring the next generation of engineers and advancing STEM education.
